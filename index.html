Research Areas

Core Research Topics

The main research directions are organized into focused pages with explanations, results, figures, and technical documentation.

01

Grid Stability

Analysis of dynamic behavior, stability margins, robustness, and operating limits in modern electric grids with increasing penetration of inverter-based resources.

Open topic →
02

Real-Time Online Inertia Measurement

Development of online methods for estimating grid inertia using controlled power steps, local frequency measurements, and filtering of inter-area oscillatory effects.

Open topic →
03

VSM Control with H-Infinity

Advanced control design for Virtual Synchronous Machines using robust control methods to improve performance, damping, and resilience under grid disturbances.

Open topic →
Research Focus

From Theory to Simulation and Laboratory Validation

The research combines analytical modeling, MATLAB/Simulink simulations, ETAP-based power system studies, and laboratory-scale experiments to evaluate stability and inertia-related behavior in practical grid scenarios.
